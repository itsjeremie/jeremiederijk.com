About

I work at the intersection of political economy, moral philosophy, and institutional theory — asking how the rules societies write shape the lives people can live.

My research draws on the tradition of classical political economy (Smith, Mill, Marx) and reconstructs it through a contemporary lens informed by behavioral economics, social ontology, and normative theory. I am particularly interested in questions of distributive justice, collective rationality, and the ethics of market design.

Before turning to academia full-time I worked in economic policy consulting across Europe, which keeps my thinking grounded in the concrete messiness of institutions.

Research & Work

Working Paper 2025

The Moral Residue of Markets

An examination of how market transactions leave behind normative traces that standard welfare economics ignores. Argues that a complete account of market outcomes must include what is owed between parties after exchange — not just what is transferred.

Research Project 2024–ongoing

Institutions as Deontic Infrastructure

A collaborative project reconceptualising economic institutions not as equilibria or conventions but as systems of status functions — drawing on Searle's social ontology and Rawlsian political philosophy to ground a new framework for institutional analysis.

Essay 2024

Against Preference Sovereignty

A philosophical critique of the assumption that revealed preferences are self-certifying. Draws on adaptive preference formation and capability theory to argue for a richer standard of economic rationality that is both empirically and normatively defensible.

Read essay →
Policy Report 2023

Housing Markets & the Limits of Price Signals

A policy-oriented investigation into why housing markets persistently fail to coordinate supply and demand even in high-information environments. Proposes a set of institutional reforms grounded in mechanism design theory.
